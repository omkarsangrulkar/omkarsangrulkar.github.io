About

Building firmware that works
when it absolutely has to

I'm an MS ECE student at the University of Colorado Boulder, specializing in microelectronics and low-power embedded design. My work sits at the boundary of hardware and software — writing production firmware for constrained devices in demanding environments.

Before grad school I spent two years at Forbes Marshall developing STM32-based industrial IoT firmware — BLE/LoRa wireless drivers, OTA update pipelines, and FFT-based vibration signal processing for predictive maintenance. I hold three IEEE publications and a granted Indian patent.

I build things to learn: a DIY mixed-signal oscilloscope from scratch on STM32F411, a Yocto-based AI rover on Raspberry Pi 5, and a distributed BLE Mesh fire & hazard detection system on EFR32. Currently open to embedded firmware internship and full-time opportunities.

EFR32 · BLE Mesh · Low-Power

Distributed Fire & Hazard Detection

4-node distributed monitoring system on EFR32BG13 using Silicon Labs GSDK 4.3.2 and a custom BLE Mesh vendor model. Coordinator node aggregates temperature and gas sensor data; dedicated alarm node drives LCD alerts. LPN deep sleep (EM2) achieves sub-mA average current for battery-operated sensor nodes in field deployment.

STM32 · GSM · OTA

Firmware OTA Update over GSM (FOTA_GSM)

Firmware over-the-air update system for STM32 using the Quectel M66 GSM module. Implements a bootloader that fetches new firmware over a cellular connection and flashes it in-field — enabling remote updates on deployed industrial IoT devices without physical access.
